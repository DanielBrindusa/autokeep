AutoKeep

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how AutoKeep handles local records, optional cloud use, manual Push to cloud and Pull from cloud actions, automatic online cleanup rules, notifications, and permanent deletion choices.

App: AutoKeep

Last updated: April 14, 2026

Support contact: daniel.c.brindusa@gmail.com

1. Local-only use is available

You can use AutoKeep locally on your device without creating an online account. If you stay in local-only mode, your private records remain saved on this device.

2. Notifications are required

AutoKeep requires notifications to be enabled before the app can be used. Notifications are used for important reminders, including the weekly Push to cloud reminder and warning messages before automatic online cleanup actions.

3. Optional online account and cloud setup

AutoKeep offers an optional online account and optional cloud use. If you choose it, you will review the cloud setup flow, choose what is allowed to sync, review and accept the required notice, create an online account, and verify your email if required.

Cloud use is optional. You can continue using the app locally if that is your preference.

4. Manual Push to cloud and Pull from cloud

AutoKeep does not automatically send your private data to the database and does not automatically pull it back. Private data moves only when you are signed in, cloud is enabled for your account, and you explicitly press:

  • Push to cloud to send selected eligible data from this device to your online account
  • Pull from cloud to bring your selected online data back to this device

No automatic private-data sync runs on app launch, sign-in, or in the background.

5. What private data may be stored online

If you enable cloud use, only the private data you selected in your sync scope is intended to be stored in your online account.

  • Vehicle profiles
  • Documents
  • Uploaded files linked to synced documents, if included in the selected sync scope
  • Reminders
  • Cloud metadata needed to keep your selected sync scope, account state, and manual sync status consistent

If a category or vehicle is not selected for cloud use, it should remain excluded from online storage.

6. Public shared resources

AutoKeep also shows shared public resources such as official links. These shared resources are separate from your private records and are not tied to your online account.

7. Sign in, sign out, and manual cloud use

If you create an online account, you can sign in again later and use manual Push to cloud or Pull from cloud. Logging out is not deletion. Logging out keeps your online data intact and keeps your local data on the device.

8. Automatic online cleanup rules

AutoKeep applies automatic online cleanup rules to online account data.

  • Inactive online account removal: if an online account stays inactive for longer than 14 months, the online account and all related online data are permanently removed automatically.
  • Expired online data removal: if online records stay expired for longer than 12 months past their expiry date, those expired online records are permanently removed automatically from the online database and cloud storage.
  • Advance warning: a notification is sent 1 week before either automatic cleanup happens.

These automatic cleanup actions cannot be stopped from inside the app. Manual Push to cloud will not upload records that are already too old for online retention.

9. Permanent deletion choices

AutoKeep offers two permanent deletion choices for signed-in users:

  • Delete online account and online data permanently: removes the online account and related online data, while keeping local device data on the phone.
  • Delete online account, online data, and local device data permanently: removes the online account and related online data, and also deletes the private AutoKeep data saved on the device.

In both cases, only the requesting user's data is affected. Shared public resources are not deleted.

10. What is deleted from the online account

When permanent online deletion is requested or when automatic online account removal applies, AutoKeep is intended to permanently delete the affected user's online account and user-owned online records, including uploaded files and online metadata tied to that user.

11. Post-delete message inside the app

After a cloud deletion completes successfully, AutoKeep shows a message on the main screen explaining what was deleted. This message remains visible until the user explicitly acknowledges it.

12. Security and access control

AutoKeep is intended to allow users to access only their own private online records. Shared public resources are handled separately from user-owned private data.
